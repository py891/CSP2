Project Overview

What the CSP is about and why we chose it.

Our CSP integrates concepts from Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Environmental Systems & Societies (ESS), and Computer Science to analyze a real-world environmental challenge and propose a solution. We combine experimental data, scientific theory, and computational tools to communicate our findings clearly.

Goal

Investigate the system scientifically and present a clear, evidence-based conclusion.

Method

Use measurements, controlled variables, and analysis (including uncertainty) across disciplines.

Subject Contributions

Click a subject to view its summary.

Physics — Madhav Gurumoorthy

The Physics component explains the system using core physical laws and quantitative modeling. It focuses on measurable variables, controlled conditions, and evaluation of experimental uncertainty.

  • Identifies key physical principles and relationships
  • Collects and analyzes experimental measurements
  • Builds graphs/models to test predictions
  • Evaluates limitations and uncertainty

Chemistry — Krishna Santhanam

The Chemistry component focuses on chemical composition and reactions that influence the system’s behavior. It connects microscopic mechanisms (molecules/ions) to macroscopic observations.

  • Analyzes chemical processes and reaction pathways
  • Explores material properties and composition
  • Connects chemical changes to environmental effects
  • Supports conclusions using chemical evidence

Biology — Keerthi Shanmugam

The Biology component investigates how living organisms and ecosystems are affected. It considers ecological interactions, biodiversity, and biological responses.

  • Examines effects on organisms and habitats
  • Considers ecosystem stability and food webs
  • Uses biological indicators to evaluate impact
  • Links findings to real-world environmental health

ESS — Aarna Karnani

The ESS component evaluates the environmental and societal significance of the problem. It focuses on sustainability, trade-offs, and long-term solutions.

  • Assesses sustainability and environmental footprint
  • Considers human impacts and stakeholders
  • Evaluates policy/solutions and feasibility
  • Connects science to real-world decision-making

Computer Science — Vetrivel Karthikeyan

The Computer Science component supports analysis through coding, data handling, and visualization. It helps communicate findings clearly and improves accuracy using computational tools.

  • Processes experimental data and organizes results
  • Creates graphs/visualizations and dashboards
  • Builds simple models or simulations (if needed)
  • Develops and maintains this website

How Everything Connects

A combined summary of the interdisciplinary approach.

By combining Physics, Chemistry, Biology, ESS, and Computer Science, we can study the issue from multiple angles: physical mechanisms, chemical processes, biological impact, sustainability trade-offs, and data-driven analysis. This integrated approach helps us form a stronger conclusion than using one subject alone.

Interdisciplinary Strength

Multiple methods confirm the same idea (triangulation) and reduce bias.

Communication

CS tools make results easier to understand through graphs, summaries, and structured pages.
